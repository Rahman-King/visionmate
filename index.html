The Navigation Gap

Visually impaired individuals face critical limitations in real-time navigation that existing solutions cannot address.

Traditional White Canes Limitations

Detect only ground-level static obstacles

Cannot identify overhead hazards or elevated dangers

Cannot detect moving vehicles

Dynamic threats remain invisible to traditional aids

Cannot identify sharp objects or fire

Critical safety hazards go undetected

The Gap

There is no real-time AI co-pilot that interprets dynamic environmental threats and provides instant spatial guidance.

Why Does This Gap Exist?

Processing visual scenes into safe, reliable, sub-second instructions is technically complex and resource-intensive.

High Cost

Specialized hardware devices like OrCam cost $4,000+, making them inaccessible to most users.

High Latency

Human or cloud-based systems require waiting time, critical seconds that could prevent accidents.

No Active Navigation

Most solutions identify objects but don't provide directional commands like "Move Left" or "Stop".

Core Problem: They inform. They don't actively guide.
